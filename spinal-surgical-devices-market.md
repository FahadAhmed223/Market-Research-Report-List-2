<p><ul><li>Fusion Devices</li><li>Non-Fusion Devices</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The spinal surgical devices market is segmented into fusion and non-fusion devices. Fusion devices, such as interbody spacers and plates, aim to stabilize the spine by encouraging bone growth between vertebrae. In contrast, non-fusion devices, which include dynamic stabilizers and artificial discs, provide stability without permanently fusing vertebrae, allowing for greater flexibility and reduced stress on adjacent segments. Both device types address various spinal conditions, offering tailored solutions for surgical interventions.</p></p>
