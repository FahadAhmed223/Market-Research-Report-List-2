<p><p>Perovskite photovoltaics are advancing solar technology, primarily categorized into two market structures: normal and inverted. The normal structure features a configuration where the perovskite layer is sandwiched between electron transport and hole transport materials, promoting efficient charge separation. Conversely, the inverted structure reverses this arrangement, with the perovskite layer placed above the hole transport material, enhancing stability and compatibility with existing silicon solar cells. Both structures aim to improve efficiency and reduce production costs in renewable energy applications.</p></p>
