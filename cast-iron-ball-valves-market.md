<p><p>The cast iron ball valve market is primarily segmented into two types: screwed and flanged cast iron ball valves. Screwed cast iron ball valves feature threaded ends for easy connection to piping systems, making them ideal for low-pressure applications and compact installations. Flanged cast iron ball valves have flanges for bolting to pipelines, offering robust stability and suitability for higher pressure systems. Both types are valued for their durability, excellent sealing capabilities, and ease of operation in various industrial applications.</p></p>
