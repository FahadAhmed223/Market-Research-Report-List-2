<p><p>The dual-chamber pacemaker market comprises two primary types: temporary and permanent pacemakers. Temporary pacemakers are designed for short-term use, typically in hospital settings, to manage transient rhythm disturbances until a more permanent solution is available. In contrast, permanent pacemakers are implanted for long-term cardiac support, providing continuous pacing to patients with chronic arrhythmias. Both types are crucial in ensuring effective heart function, with permanent options often offering advanced features for improved patient outcomes.</p></p>
